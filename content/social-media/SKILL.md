# Social Media

**Requires:** clearvision-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

Clear Vision's social media content serves one purpose: put David in front of the right people often enough that when they need what he offers, he is who they think of. Not viral. Not clever for its own sake. Just consistent, honest, and useful.

This skill generates post copy, short series, and caption content for Clear Vision's channels. Every post sounds like David -- direct, results-focused, occasionally warm -- not like a marketing agency's social team.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Content type: single post, short series (3-5 posts), or repurposed content (from a client story, a win, a question David gets asked a lot)
- Platform: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, or all three
- Topic or angle (see categories below, or describe what you want)

**Helpful:**
- A specific client win or story to build around (anonymized)
- A specific pain point David hears often
- A seasonal angle (slow season, busy season, end of year, new year)
- Whether this is awareness content (new audiences) or trust-building content (people who already know David)

---

## Content Categories

Use these to decide what to post and when. A healthy content mix rotates through all four.

**1. Problem / Pain Point Posts**
Name a problem small business owners, churches, or coaches recognize. Don't sell. Just name it honestly. These earn engagement because the reader feels seen.

*Examples of angles:*
- "Most small businesses don't lose leads because they're bad at their job. They lose them because no one followed up."
- "Your Google reviews are what new customers read before they ever call you. Most businesses aren't asking for them."
- "A church can preach a great sermon to a first-time guest and never hear from them again because no one followed up Monday morning."

**2. Results / Win Posts**
Share a specific, anonymized result. Not a testimonial -- a story. What was the problem, what changed, what happened.

*Format:* Problem → What changed → Result. Three paragraphs, each two sentences or less.

**3. Education / How-It-Works Posts**
Explain one small piece of how CV Pro Tools or the Clear Vision system works. Not a feature list -- one idea, explained simply, connected to a benefit.

*Examples of angles:*
- "Here's what a missed call text-back actually does -- and why it works."
- "Why birthday campaigns aren't just for restaurants."
- "The difference between a CRM and an inbox -- and why most small businesses need both."

**4. Credibility / Trust Posts**
David has used CV Pro Tools himself for five years at the BSU. He built Clear Vision in 2002. He has worked with churches, coaches, and local businesses. He is not selling something he hasn't vetted. These posts build trust by being honest about who he is and what he knows.

*Examples of angles:*
- David's own use of the system at the BSU -- what it tracks, what it automates, what it has changed.
- 20+ years of working with local businesses and churches in the Ozarks.
- "I don't sell tools I haven't used myself."

## Step 1: Identify the Platform and Format

### Facebook
Slightly longer posts are fine (150-300 words). Can use short paragraphs with line breaks. Good for stories and educational content. Engagement comes from comments -- write content that invites a response.

### Instagram
Shorter and more visual-dependent. Caption supports the image, doesn't replace it. Keep captions under 150 words. Use a hook in the first line -- it's the only line visible before "more." Hashtags are minimal (3-5, relevant, not gimmicky).

### LinkedIn
Most professional of the three. Best for content aimed at coaches, ministry leaders, church staff, and business owners who make decisions. Slightly longer form is fine. First line must hook -- it shows as the preview. No hollow inspiration. Real insight or real results.

### All three
When posting across platforms, the core message is the same but the format adapts. Generate three versions: LinkedIn (professional, insight-forward), Facebook (story or education, slightly warmer), Instagram (hook + short body + CTA).

---

## Step 2: Generate the Post(s)

Follow these rules for every post:

**The hook is everything.** The first sentence has to earn the second. On every platform, the first line is what determines whether someone keeps reading. Write the first line last. Make it the sharpest thing on the page.

**One idea per post.** Not a feature list. Not "here are five reasons." One thing, said well, with a clear so-what.

**Plain language.** If a word sounds like it belongs in a brochure, replace it. "Help" not "support." "Use" not "leverage." "Customers" not "clients" for small business content. "People" not "individuals."

**End with something.** Either a question that invites engagement, a specific call to action, or a statement that gives someone something to think about. Never trail off.

**No em dashes. No banned phrases.** See clearvision-foundation.

---

### SINGLE POST FORMAT

**[Platform]**

[Hook -- one line. The sharpest version of the idea.]

[Body -- 2-4 short paragraphs. Problem → insight or result. Each paragraph 1-3 sentences. Line breaks between paragraphs for readability on mobile.]

[Close -- a question, a CTA, or a statement that lands.]

[Hashtags if Instagram: 3-5 only. Relevant, not trending-chasing.]

---

### SHORT SERIES FORMAT (3-5 posts)

A series on one topic, posted over one to two weeks. Each post stands alone but builds on the others.

**Series structure:**
- Post 1: Name the problem. Make the reader feel seen.
- Post 2: Why it happens. Education without a sales pitch.
- Post 3: What the solution looks like. Concrete, specific, real.
- Post 4 (optional): A result or story. Proof without overselling.
- Post 5 (optional): The next step. A gentle, specific CTA.

---

### REPURPOSED CONTENT FORMAT

Taking a client win, a conversation, or a common question and turning it into a post.

**From a client win:**
Strip the identifying details. Keep the specific result. Format: "A [business type] we work with was [problem]. We set up [one-sentence description of what changed]. [Result in concrete terms -- number of reviews, leads recovered, specific outcome]."

**From a question David gets asked often:**
"I get asked this a lot: [question]. Here's the honest answer: [2-3 paragraphs]."

**From a conversation:**
"Had a conversation this week with a [business owner / pastor / coach] who said [paraphrase -- not a quote]. It got me thinking about [insight]. [Develop it in 2-3 short paragraphs]."

---

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering any post:

- First line is a hook -- not an introduction, not a greeting, not "Did you know..."
- One idea per post, fully developed
- No feature lists without benefit translation
- Plain language throughout
- Ends with something (question, CTA, or a statement that sticks)
- No em dashes
- No banned phrases from clearvision-foundation
- Length is appropriate for the platform
- Nothing sounds like it was written by a marketing agency
