# Proposal Generator

**Requires:** clearvision-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

A proposal is not a brochure. It answers three questions the prospect is already asking: what will this do for me, what does it cost, and what happens next. Every proposal produced by this skill answers all three -- in that order, clearly, without burying the price or overselling the outcome.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Client name and business name
- Business type / industry
- Proposal stage: FID, Fast Start, or Core R6
- Any specific pain points or context from your conversation

**Helpful:**
- How they came in (referral, networking, cold outreach)
- What you already know about their situation
- Any wins from the current system (if this is an upgrade proposal)
- Team size (affects Fast Start and Core R6 pricing)

---

## Step 1: Identify the Right Proposal Stage

**FID ($97-197/month):** New client, no prior HL experience, skeptical about committing. Or any warm prospect where you want a low-friction yes. Lead with the quick win, not the full system.

**Fast Start ($197-497/month):** FID client who has seen a win and is ready for more. Or a warmer, more sophisticated prospect who is ready to move past the entry level. Requires a specific win or readiness signal to open with.

**Core R6 ($297-697/month):** Fast Start client with momentum. Or an experienced prospect who is ready to buy the full system. See r6-fast-start repo for full Core R6 documentation -- use that as the reference for Stage 3 proposals.

---

## Step 2: Generate the Proposal

Produce a complete, ready-to-send proposal document. Length should match the stage: FID proposals are short and direct (one page). Fast Start proposals are slightly longer. Core R6 proposals can be more detailed.

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering the proposal:

- Price is visible and not buried
- The specific problem named is real for this business -- not generic
- The deliverables list is calibrated to what this business actually needs (not a copy-paste of every feature)
- One clear, specific next step at the end
- No em dashes anywhere
- No banned phrases from clearvision-foundation
- Length is appropriate: FID proposals are one page, no longer
- Nothing sounds like a vendor brochure
