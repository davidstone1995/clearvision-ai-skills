# FID Pitch Generator

**Requires:** clearvision-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

The FID pitch is a 5-10 minute conversation that gets a small business owner to say yes to a $97-197/month package. It does not require a deep audit, a framework presentation, or a long sales process. It requires three things: a clear problem the prospect recognizes, a simple solution they can understand, and a specific next step.

This skill takes what you know about a prospect and produces a pitch built around their world -- their industry, their likely pain points, and the objections they are most likely to raise.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Business name and/or owner name
- Business type / industry (e.g., HVAC, pest control, landscaping, church, coaching practice)

**Helpful:**
- How you know them (networking, referral, cold outreach, social media)
- Anything you already know about their situation (no Google reviews, slow season, new business, etc.)
- Suggested price point (if you have one in mind)

If you only have a business type, that is enough to generate a full pitch.

---

## Step 1: Identify the Right Entry Point

Before generating the pitch, determine which pain point to lead with. Every business has gaps -- pick the one most likely to land for this prospect.

**Missed calls / lead recovery:** Best for service businesses with phone-based inquiry (HVAC, plumbers, pest control, contractors). They know they miss calls. They rarely have a system to recover them.

**Reviews:** Best for any business where reputation matters locally (restaurants, home services, salons, contractors). Almost no one has an automated review request system.

**Existing customer reactivation:** Best for businesses with a past customer base that isn't being worked (gyms, salons, shops, restaurants, seasonal services). The leads are already there -- they just aren't being touched.

**Guest/visitor follow-up:** Best for churches and ministries. First-time guests slip through. CV Pro Tools captures them and follows up automatically.

**Client pipeline and follow-up:** Best for coaches, consultants, and solo practitioners. Leads come in, nothing happens. The system fixes that.

## Step 2: Generate the Pitch Package

Produce all five components. Keep every script in David's voice: direct, warm, plain language. No marketing speak.

---

### COMPONENT 1: One-Sentence Opener

Calibrate to the prospect's world. The standard opener works but tailor it when you know enough to do so.

**Standard:**
> "I help small businesses like yours make sure no lead slips through the cracks -- and that your best customers keep coming back and sending referrals. We do it with a simple automated system that runs 24/7 without you having to think about it."

**For home services / trades:**
> "I help [trade] businesses stop losing leads to missed calls -- and build a system that automatically follows up, requests reviews, and keeps past customers coming back."

**For churches:**
> "I help churches capture first-time guests and follow up with them automatically -- so no one who walks through the door for the first time disappears before they can connect."

**For coaches / solo practitioners:**
> "I help coaches and consultants set up an automated system that follows up with leads, books appointments, and keeps the pipeline moving -- without them having to manually chase anyone."

After the opener: ask a question. Do not keep talking.

---

### COMPONENT 2: Discovery Questions (pick 1-2)

Choose the questions most relevant to this prospect's likely pain point. Never ask all three in the same conversation.

**Lead recovery:**
> "When someone calls your business and doesn't reach you -- what happens? Do they hear from you again automatically, or does that lead usually just disappear?"

*Most small business owners admit the lead disappears. That's the opening.*

**Reviews:**
> "How many Google reviews do you have right now? And do you have a system that automatically asks happy customers to leave one after every job -- or is it more hit-or-miss?"

*Almost no one has an automated system. Another opening.*

**Existing customers:**
> "Do you have a way to reach out to your existing customers automatically -- birthday messages, seasonal offers, that kind of thing? Or does that tend to fall through the cracks?"

*Nearly always the answer is no.*

**For churches:**
> "When a first-time guest visits on Sunday, what happens next? Do they get a follow-up from the church automatically, or does that depend on someone remembering to reach out?"

---

### COMPONENT 3: The Pivot

After they confirm the problem, pivot to the solution. Keep it short. Match the price to the prospect.

> "That's really common -- and it's completely solvable. What we do is set up a simple automated system for businesses like yours that handles all of that: missed calls get a text back instantly, happy customers get a review request automatically, and your existing customers hear from you on birthdays and with seasonal messages.
>
> It takes us about a week to set up, and it runs on its own from there. Most of our clients see their first result -- a new review, a recovered lead, a reactivated customer -- within the first 30 days.
>
> It's [price] per month, and there's no long-term contract. Would it be worth a quick conversation to see if it makes sense for your business?"

**Pricing guidance:**

| Situation | Price |
|---|---|
| Solo operator, skeptical, first conversation | $97/mo |
| Established business, warm referral | $147/mo |
| Business with a team or prior tool experience | $197/mo |

When in doubt, start at $147. Easy to justify, room to grow.

---

### COMPONENT 4: Objection Responses

Include the two or three objections most likely for this prospect type. Use David's voice -- direct, honest, no pressure.

**"I already have someone helping with marketing."**
> "Good -- then this will either work alongside what they're doing or fill in the gaps they're not covering. The missed call text-back and review automation are things most marketing companies don't set up. Want to see if there's a gap worth filling?"

**"I don't have the budget right now."**
> "Fair enough. Let me ask -- what does a missed lead cost you? If one new customer is worth [their typical job value], and we're recovering leads that would otherwise just disappear, the system usually pays for itself in the first month. For $[price], it's really a question of whether you can afford to leave that on the table."

**"I need to think about it."**
> "Of course. What's the main thing you'd want to think through? I'd rather answer any questions now than have you filling in blanks on your own later."

*After they answer: address it directly, then ask again. "Does that help? If so, want to get started?"*

**"I've tried stuff like this before and it didn't work."**
> "That's actually pretty common -- most of those tools require someone to manage them. This one runs on its own once it's set up. The missed call text-back doesn't need you to do anything. The review request sends automatically. What tripped you up last time?"

*Listen. Address the specific thing that broke. Then proceed.*

---

### COMPONENT 5: The Close

Simple. Specific. Book something before the conversation ends.

> "Here's what I'd suggest: let me get you set up with a quick onboarding call -- takes about 30 minutes. We'll go through your business, get access to the tools we need, and have everything running within a week. When does your schedule look like this week or next?"

Book a specific time. Send a calendar invite before you hang up (or before you close the message thread).

**After they say yes:**
1. Confirm the price and package
2. Collect payment or send a Stripe invoice through CV Pro Tools
3. Send the FID welcome email
4. Schedule the setup call within 5 business days
5. Start the FID Delivery Checklist

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering the pitch:

- Opener is calibrated to this prospect's industry and situation
- Discovery questions match the most likely pain point for this business type
- Pivot script has the correct price filled in (not a placeholder)
- Objections selected are the ones most likely for this prospect
- Nothing sounds like a marketing email or a pitch deck
- No em dashes anywhere
- No banned phrases from clearvision-foundation
