# Client Onboarding

**Requires:** clearvision-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

The first week sets the tone for everything that follows. A client who feels informed, cared for, and confident in the first seven days becomes a client who stays, upgrades, and refers. This skill produces the three documents that make that happen: the welcome email, the setup checklist David uses internally, and the what-to-expect document the client receives.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Client name and business name
- Package: FID or Fast Start
- Business type / industry

**Helpful:**
- Setup call date and time (if already scheduled)
- Any specific notes from the sales conversation
- Whether they need a website built (FID only)

---

## Step 1: Identify the Package and Context

**FID clients:** Moving fast. The goal is to get one visible win in 30 days. They may be skeptical. The onboarding communication should be warm, direct, and focused on what's happening -- not on educating them about the platform.

**Fast Start clients:** Either upgrading from FID (they already know the system) or coming in at a slightly higher level. Onboarding is more expansive -- the Conversations inbox, SMS list, call tracking, and Yext are all being configured. The communication acknowledges their existing context if they're an upgrade.

---

## Step 2: Generate the Onboarding Package

Produce all three components. The welcome email and what-to-expect document go to the client. The setup checklist stays with David.

---

### COMPONENT 1: Welcome Email

Sent immediately after payment is confirmed and the setup call is scheduled.

**Subject: You're in -- here's what happens next**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Welcome aboard. We're setting up your system now and we'll be ready to walk through everything together on your setup call.
>
> Before we talk, it would help to have these ready:
> - Your Google Business Profile login (or the ability to add us as a manager)
> - Your Facebook Business Page access
> - The phone number your customers call
>
> If you can't pull those together before the call, no problem -- we'll work through it together.
>
> Your setup call: [Date / Time / Zoom or phone link]
>
> Any questions before we talk? Just reply here.
>
> David Stone
> Clear Vision Design and Marketing
> clearvision.marketing

**For Fast Start upgrade clients:** Add one sentence above the closing: "Everything you have running now stays exactly in place -- we're building on top of it."

**What to avoid:**
- Anything that sounds like a marketing email
- Lists of features they're getting (save that for the what-to-expect document)
- Asking them to do anything beyond gathering the three items listed

### COMPONENT 2: Setup Checklist (David's internal use)

**[Business Name] -- [FID / Fast Start] Setup**

**Before the setup call:**
- [ ] Create client's HL sub-account
- [ ] Apply [FID / Fast Start] snapshot
- [ ] Confirm snapshot applied correctly (workflows, campaigns, forms present)
- [ ] Send welcome email
- [ ] Prep setup call agenda

**Collect on the setup call:**
- [ ] Google Business Profile access (add as Manager)
- [ ] Facebook Business Page access
- [ ] Primary business phone number (for Smart Number routing)
- [ ] Business hours
- [ ] Website URL (or confirm website build is needed)
- [ ] 3-5 common customer FAQs
- [ ] Birthday offer idea
- [ ] Reactivation offer idea (what to say to a customer who hasn't been in touch in 90 days)

**Week 1 -- in priority order:**

1. Missed call text-back (Day 1 -- fastest win)
   - [ ] Purchase / activate Smart Number
   - [ ] Route to client's primary line
   - [ ] Configure text-back message (business name, friendly, under 160 characters)
   - [ ] Test: call Smart Number, don't answer, confirm text arrives within 60 seconds

2. Review request automation (Day 1-2)
   - [ ] Connect Google Business Profile
   - [ ] Set trigger (appointment completed, invoice paid, or form submission)
   - [ ] Customize message with business name
   - [ ] Test: manually trigger with test contact, confirm SMS/email arrive
   - [ ] Set up review monitoring alerts

3. Web chat + AI bot (Day 2-3)
   - [ ] Configure widget (business name, greeting, offline response)
   - [ ] Build AI bot knowledge base (name, address, hours, top services, FAQs, booking CTA)
   - [ ] Embed widget on website
   - [ ] Test on mobile and desktop

4. Website (if needed) (Day 2-4)
   - [ ] Build core pages: Home, About, Services, Contact
   - [ ] Embed web chat widget
   - [ ] Add lead capture form on Contact page
   - [ ] Connect domain or provide subdomain
   - [ ] Confirm mobile-responsive

5. Birthday funnel + reactivation (Day 3-4)
   - [ ] Import customer list into CRM
   - [ ] Tag contacts as "Customer"
   - [ ] Configure birthday campaign (offer from setup call, test with today's DOB)
   - [ ] Configure reactivation campaign (lapsed threshold, offer, **get client approval before first send**)

6. AI voice inbound (Day 4-5)
   - [ ] Configure AI voice greeting (include business name)
   - [ ] Add FAQ responses (same content as AI bot)
   - [ ] Connect to booking calendar if applicable
   - [ ] Test: call Smart Number, let AI answer, confirm correct responses

**Fast Start additions (if applicable):**
- [ ] Connect all channels to Conversations inbox (SMS, email, Facebook, Instagram)
- [ ] Set up SMS list building opt-in
- [ ] Configure call tracking
- [ ] Set up Text-to-Pay
- [ ] Connect Yext (verify business info is accurate first)

**End of Week 1 quality check:**
- [ ] Missed call text-back tested and confirmed
- [ ] Review request workflow tested and confirmed
- [ ] Web chat visible on website (desktop + mobile)
- [ ] AI bot answers common questions correctly
- [ ] Birthday campaign tested
- [ ] Reactivation campaign approved before any sends
- [ ] Client has HL login and knows where to find Conversations inbox
- [ ] "System is live" email sent to client

### COMPONENT 3: What to Expect (client-facing)

A one-page document sent at the end of Week 1 when setup is complete, or shared at the setup call if the client asks questions about the system.

---

**What to Expect from Your CV Pro Tools System**
Clear Vision Design and Marketing | clearvision.marketing

---

**What's running automatically**

Your system is set up to do the work in the background so you don't have to think about it.

[FID list:]
- Missed call text-back: Anyone who calls and doesn't reach you gets an automatic text within seconds, keeping the conversation alive.
- Review requests: Every time a customer completes a transaction, they get an automatic request to leave a Google review. You don't have to ask.
- Web chat: Your website now captures visitors who aren't ready to call. They can ask questions and get answers anytime.
- Birthday campaign: Customers hear from you automatically on their birthday with a personalized offer.
- Reactivation campaign: Customers who haven't been in touch in [X days] get a message from you -- you just approved what it says.

[Fast Start additions:]
- Conversations inbox: Every SMS, email, Facebook, and Instagram message comes into one place. Nothing gets missed.
- Call tracking: You can see exactly how many calls you're getting and whether they're being answered.
- SMS list: Customers and prospects can opt in to hear from you by text. You own that list.

**What you need to do**

Very little. The system runs on its own.

Check your Conversations inbox in HL regularly -- that's where everything comes in. When a customer responds to an automated message, it shows up there. That's a warm lead or an active customer. Respond to those.

**What you'll see in the first 30 days**

Most clients see their first result within two weeks: a new review, a recovered lead, a reactivated customer, or a web chat conversation. We'll check in with you at the 30-day mark to walk through what the system has done.

**Questions or issues**

Reply to any email from Clear Vision or reach David directly at [contact info]. If something looks wrong in the system, let us know right away.

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering the package:

- Welcome email is under 200 words and sounds like David, not a template
- Setup checklist is calibrated to the correct package (FID vs Fast Start)
- What-to-expect document only lists what's actually being set up for this client
- No em dashes
- No banned phrases from clearvision-foundation
- Nothing sounds like vendor documentation
