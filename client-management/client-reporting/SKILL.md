# Client Reporting

**Requires:** clearvision-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

Monthly reporting serves two purposes: it proves the system is working, and it builds the case for the next conversation -- whether that's retention, an upgrade, or a referral ask. A good monthly report leads with the win, backs it up with the numbers, and ends with one clear forward step.

The numbers matter. But one specific, concrete win is worth more than a summary of metrics. Find that win first. Build the report around it.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Client name and business name
- Package: FID or Fast Start
- Month being reported
- Key metrics (provide whatever you have -- the skill will work with what's available)

**Helpful:**
- Number of new Google reviews and current star rating
- Missed call text-backs sent / any that converted to conversations or bookings
- Web chat conversations started
- Birthday messages sent / responses
- Reactivation campaign results
- Fast Start: Conversations inbox volume, SMS list size, call tracking numbers, Yext status
- Any notable moments (a specific customer story, a big review, a recovered sale)
- Current stage progression signals (is the client logging in? asking about more features? ready to talk about upgrading?)

If you only have partial numbers, that is fine. Report on what you have. Don't fabricate results.

---

## Step 1: Find the Best Win

Before writing anything, identify the single best result from the month. This is the lead of the report.

**What counts as a win:**
- A specific new review (especially a 5-star with a comment)
- A missed call that turned into a booking or sale
- A reactivated customer who came back
- A birthday message that generated a response or purchase
- A web chat conversation that converted
- A measurable increase in any key metric month-over-month

If there is no single standout result, lead with the most relevant metric and be honest: "The system ran consistently this month -- here's what it did."

Never fabricate a win or dress up modest results as something they aren't. Long-term trust matters more than a good-looking report.

---

## Step 2: Generate the Report

Produce a ready-to-send monthly results email. Concise. Specific. Ends with one forward step.

---

### MONTHLY RESULTS EMAIL

**Subject: [Business Name] -- [Month] Results**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Here's your [Month] summary.
>
> **The highlight:** [One sentence describing the best result from the month. Specific. Real. Example: "Your missed call text-back recovered a conversation this month that turned into a booking -- that's a direct return on the system."]
>
> **What the system did this month:**
>
> - Reviews: [X] new reviews / [current star rating] average. [Note any trend: up from last month, or holding steady.]
> - Missed call text-backs: [X] sent. [Any notable conversations or bookings that came from them.]
> - Web chat: [X] conversations started.
> - Birthday messages: [X] sent. [Any responses noted.]
> - Reactivation: [X] messages sent / [X] responses.
>
> [Fast Start additions if applicable:]
> - Conversations inbox: [X] total messages handled across all channels.
> - SMS list: [X] contacts. [Growth from last month if relevant.]
> - Call tracking: [X] calls tracked. [Answer rate or notable trend.]
> - Yext: [Status -- "All listings accurate" or note any corrections made.]
>
> **Where things stand:** [One short paragraph. Is the system performing as expected? Is there anything to adjust? Is the client ready for the next conversation? Keep it honest. If something underperformed, say so and explain why or what to do about it.]
>
> **One thing to watch:** [Optional -- flag a specific metric or trend worth paying attention to next month.]
>
> Let me know if you have any questions, or if you'd like to walk through these numbers together.
>
> David Stone
> Clear Vision Design and Marketing
> clearvision.marketing

### UPGRADE SIGNAL SECTION (internal -- not sent to client)

After writing the report, assess the client's upgrade readiness. This is for David's use -- not included in the client email.

**FID clients -- Fast Start readiness:**
- [ ] Has a visible win they can point to
- [ ] Is logging into HL at least weekly
- [ ] Has 20+ contacts in the CRM
- [ ] Is using the Conversations inbox
- [ ] Is asking about additional capabilities
- [ ] 45 days have passed since FID launch

If two or more boxes are checked, include an upgrade conversation note: "Based on this month's results, [Client Name] looks ready for the Fast Start conversation. The [specific win] is a strong opening."

**Fast Start clients -- Core R6 readiness:**
- [ ] Core systems running and producing consistent results
- [ ] 50+ contacts in the CRM
- [ ] Client is clearly benefiting and trusts the system
- [ ] David is ready to present the R6 framework

If readiness signals are present, note it: "[Client Name] may be ready for the Core R6 conversation. See r6-fast-start repo for the full framework before that call."

---

### 30-DAY FIRST CHECK-IN (use instead of full monthly report for first check-in)

The first check-in is different. The client is still forming their opinion of the system. Lead with what worked. Be present. Make them feel confident.

**Subject: 30-Day Check-In -- [Business Name]**

> Hi [Name],
>
> It's been 30 days since we launched your system. Here's what it's done.
>
> [Best result from the first 30 days -- one sentence, specific and real.]
>
> Here's the full picture:
>
> [Same metrics format as monthly report, but shorter -- this is a conversation-starter, not a full report.]
>
> I'd love to walk through this with you. [Schedule a call, or ask if they have questions.] A lot of clients have questions at the 30-day mark once they've had a chance to see the system in action.
>
> David

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering the report:

- Best win is identified and leads the email
- All numbers provided are real (no fabricated metrics)
- "Where things stand" paragraph is honest -- not spin
- Upgrade signal section is completed internally if applicable
- Email is under 300 words (monthly summary) or under 200 words (30-day check-in)
- No em dashes
- No banned phrases from clearvision-foundation
- Nothing sounds like an automated marketing report
